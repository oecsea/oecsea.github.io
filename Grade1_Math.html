  1. Number Sense and Numeration:
    1. Quantity Relationships
      1. represent, compare, and order whole numbers to 50, using a variety of tools and contexts
      2. read and print in words whole numbers to ten, using meaningful contexts
      3. demonstrate, using concrete materials, the concept of conservation of number
      4. relate numbers to the anchors of 5 and 10
      5. identify and describe various coins, using coin manipulatives or drawings, and state their value
      6. represent money amounts to 20¢, through investigation using coin manipulatives
      7. estimate the number of objects in a set,and check by counting
      8. compose and decompose numbers up to20 in a variety of ways, using concrete materials
      9. divide whole objects into parts and identify and describe, through investigation,equal-sized parts of the whole, using fractional names
    2. Counting
      1. demonstrate, using concrete materials, the concept of one-to-one correspondence between number and objects when counting
      2. count forward by 1’s, 2’s, 5’s, and 10’s to 100, using a variety of tools and strategies
      3. count backwards by 1’s from 20 and any number less than 20
      4. count backwards from 20 by 2’s and 5’s,using a variety of tools
      5. use ordinal numbers to thirty-first in meaningful contexts
    3. Operational Sense
      1. solve a variety of problems involving the addition and subtraction of whole numbers to 20, using concrete materials and drawings
      2. solve problems involving the addition and subtraction of single-digit whole numbers,using a variety of mental strategies
      3. add and subtract money amounts to 10¢,using coin manipulatives and drawings.
  2. Measurement
    1. Attributes, Units, and Measurement Sense
      1. demonstrate an understanding of the use of non-standard units of the same size for measuring
      2. estimate, measure, and record lengths, heights, and distances
      3. construct, using a variety of strategies,tools for measuring lengths, heights, and distances in non-standard units
      4. estimate, measure, and describe area, through investigation using non-standard units
      5. estimate, measure, and describe the capacity and/or mass of an object, through investigation using non-standard units
      6. estimate, measure, and describe the passage of time, through investigation using non-standard units
      7. read demonstration digital and analogue clocks, and use them to identify bench-mark times
      8. name the months of the year in order, and read the date on a calendar;
      9. relate temperature to experiences of the seasons
    2. Measurement Relationships
      1. compare two or three objects using measurable attributes,and describe the objects using relative terms
      2. compare and order objects by their linear measurements, using the same non-standard unit
      3. use the metre as a benchmark for measuring length, and compare the metre with non-standard units
      4. describe, through investigation using con-crete materials, the relationship between the size of a unit and the number of units needed to measure length
  3. Geometry and Spatial Sense
    1. Geometric Properties
      1. Identify and describe common two-dimensional shapes and sort and classify them by their attributes, using concrete materials and pictorial representations
      2. trace and identify the two-dimensional faces of three-dimensional figures, using concrete models
      3. identify and describe common three-dimensional figures and sort and classify them by their attributes, using concrete materials and pictorial representations
      4. describe similarities and differences between an everyday object and a three-dimensional figure
      5. locate shapes in the environment that have symmetry, and describe the symmetry
    2. Geometric Relationships
      1. compose patterns, pictures, and designs,using common two-dimensional shapes
      2. identify and describe shapes within other shapes
      3. build three-dimensional structures using concrete materials, and describe the two-dimensional shapes the structures contain
      4. cover outline puzzles with two-dimensional shapes
    3. Location and Movement
      1. describe the relative locations of objects or people using positional language
      2. describe the relative locations of objects on concrete maps created in the classroom
      3. create symmetrical designs and pictures,using concrete materials
  4. Patterning and Algebra
    1. Patterns and Relationships
      1. identify, describe, and extend, through investigation, geometric repeating patterns involving one attribute
      2. identify and extend, through investigation,numeric repeating patterns
      3. describe numeric repeating patterns in a hundreds chart;
      4. identify a rule for a repeating pattern
      5. create a repeating pattern involving one attribute
      6. represent a given repeating pattern in a variety of ways
    2. Expressions and Equality
      1. create a set in which the number of objects is greater than, less than, or equal to the number of objects in a given set;
      2. demonstrate examples of equality, through investigation, using a “balance” model
      3. determine, through investigation using a“balance” model and whole numbers to10, the number of identical objects that must be added or subtracted to establish equality
  5. Data Management and Probability
    1. Collection and Organization of Data
      1. demonstrate an ability to organize objects into categories by sorting and classifying objects using one attribute, and by describing informal sorting experiences
      2. collect and organize primary data that is categorical,and display the data using one-to-one correspondence, prepared templates of concrete graphs and pictographs, and a variety of recording methods
    2. Data Relationships
      1. read primary data presented in concrete graphs and pictographs, and describe the data using comparative language
      2. pose and answer questions about collected data
    3. Probability
      1. describe the likelihood that everyday events will occur, using mathematical language
