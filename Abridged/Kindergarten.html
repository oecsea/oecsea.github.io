Ontario Education Curriculum - Specific Expectations - Abridged - Kindergarten

Official document
Unabridged
  1. Communication
    • Sing happy-birthday with a rhythm
    • Communicate ideas with language, face, hands, body, art, and signs to exchange ideas, end feelings
    • Vary tone of voice when dramatizing
    • Identify feelings in others
    • Tell a story in the correct sequence
    • Rhyme and make new words from prefixes, suffixes, and phonemes
  2. Independence
    • Self-reliance; put on cloths, feed oneself
    • Responsibility; clean up after oneself, self-control, following policy, persistence
    • Know when to ask for help
    • Will to try new things
    • Imaginary play
  3. Sociability
    • Positive choice of words, tone of voice, and gestures
    • Constructive criticism
    • Share; take turns talking, listening, helping, entering
  4. Solving problems with guessing and checking, strategies
  5. Understand diversity in language, culture, memory, perspective, and ideas
  6. Health
    • Effects of physical exertion, healthy food(nutritionists, no additives), and sleep on mind and body
    • Hygiene; wash hands and body, clean cloths, cut hair & nails
    • Harmful substances
  7. Endurance
  8. Coordination
    • Improvement through practice
    • Spatial awareness
    • Balance
    • Flexibility
    • Arts and crafts
  9. Text
    • Guess text meaning from images and probable sentences
  10. Literacy communication
    • Identify letters and their sounds, ones own name, exit sign, a word or two
    • Use prepared pictures to convey ideas in-place of text (grocery list)
    • Phonics; letters and sounds relationships
    • Copy text from a picture dictionary
  11. Literacy by being read to
    • Reading enthusiasm; Identify favorite stories
    • Conventions; left to right, front to back, letters, words, sentences
    • Respond to aspects of stories; paint, draw, build
    • Make predictions
    • Identify connections with other experiences
    • Recall non-fiction information
  12. Media text
    • Comment on animation and other media
  13. Strategize (question, plan, predict, observe)
    • Before, during, and after investigation
    • Select and use materials to investigate / explore
    • Communicate results from individual and group investigations
  14. Object awareness
    • Describe and question natural occurrences
    • Come up with ways to sort / classify groups of living and non-living things
  15. Numbers
    • Number line; measuring tape
    • Quantity and equality; who got more treats
    • Subitize(know quantity without counting) to 5 (how many fingers)
    • Numbers to indicate quantity, order, distance, id
    • Money use
    • Count objects in groups of up to 5, factor 10 with colored objects
  16. Measure (using non-standard units) (length, mass, volume, area, temperature, time)
  17. Objects attributes (describe, sort, classify, build, move)
    • Symmetry, faces, edges, corners, angles, size
    • Spatial relations; above/below, in/out, forward/backward, perspective, flips/reflections, slides/translations, turns/rotations
    • Dimensions; cube, square, line, point
  18. Patterns
    • Recognize, describe, extend, translate, create, identify smallest unit, and compare
    • eg; morning-noon-night, four seasons, leaves on a plant, clothing, floor tiles, words in a book or poem, calendar or schedule, beat or rhythm in songs
  19. Probability
    • answer questions by collecting data, and make a graph (favorites of a group, average items in a space)
    • interpret graphs and draw conclusions; We need more food to feed all these people

  21. respond to (drama, dance, music, and visual arts)
  22. art (communicate thoughts, feelings, ideas)
